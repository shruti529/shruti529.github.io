Data modeling

Kimball STAR schema

Kimball STAR schema has FACT and Dimension tables.

FACT tables

  • FACT tables represent business events.
  • FACT tables are normalized.
  • FACT tables have only numbers and foreign keys to dimension tables.
  • They don’t reference other FACT tables.
  • FACT tables are immutable and append-only.
  • FACT tables are typically narrow and long. They don’t have as many columns but they have a lot of rows.
  • FACT table rows have a 1:1 relationship to events. Atomic grain refers to lowest level at which data is captured by a business process. [Atomic grains are recommended because data can be rolled up for summary grains but not vice-versa.]

Dimension tables

  • Dimension tables provide reference data, context and attributes for events stored in FACT tables.
  • Dimension tables are wide and short.
  • Dimension tables are denormlized. They can contain duplicated data.
  • Dimension tables describe the who, what, where, when, why and how of the events.
